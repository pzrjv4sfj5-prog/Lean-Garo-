## 1. Negative Forms вЂ” Present Negative (`-ja`)
`ChaВ·ja` = does not eat / do not eat / is not eating.

## 2. The Meaning of `-jaha` (CORRECTED вЂ” supersedes 2026-07-01 Rule 17)
Earlier described as "did not eat" вЂ” **this was wrong**. Final clarification:

> "The 'ha' ending when used with 'ja' may be understood as termination or discontinuation of an action." вЂ” Thangseng

Meaning: the subject **was** doing the action previously but **has stopped**. NOT simple past. NOT "did not."

- `ChaВ·jaha` = has stopped eating / not eating anymore
- `Dakjaha` = has stopped doing

**Do NOT translate `-jaha` as "did not" or "never did."**

## 3. Completed Actions (`-manaha`) вЂ” NEW
> "'manaha' has the meaning of completion of something." вЂ” Thangseng

- `ChaВ·manaha` = ate / has eaten / has done eating
- `Ringmanaha` = drank / has done drinking

## 4. `-jaha` vs `-manaha`
- `ChaВ·jaha` = termination of an ongoing action (stopped eating, not eating anymore)
- `ChaВ·manaha` = completion of an action (ate, has eaten, finished eating)
Never interchangeable.

## 5. Future Negative (`-jawa`) вЂ” reconfirms existing Rule 16
`Verb+jawa` = will not. `Anga chaВ·jawa` = I will not eat. `Ringjawa` = I will not drink. `ReВ·jawa` = I will not go.
